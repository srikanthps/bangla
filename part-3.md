# Part 3 – Speaking in Complete Sentences

# Welcome

So far you've learned how to pronounce Bangla and have built a foundation of useful vocabulary.

Now comes the exciting part—speaking in complete sentences.

Many language courses introduce grammar at this stage.

We won't.

Instead, you'll learn **sentence patterns**. Think of them as templates. Once you know a pattern, you can replace one or two words and create dozens of new sentences.

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to introduce yourself, talk about your family, describe things, ask simple questions, and express your needs.

---

# Sentence Pattern 1

## I am...

**Pattern**

```
Ami ...
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami Rahul. | I am Rahul. |
| Ami Srikanth. | I am Srikanth. |
| Ami chhatro. | I am a student. |
| Ami daktar. | I am a doctor. |
| Ami engineer. | I am an engineer. |

Notice that Bangla usually doesn't need the word **"am"** in simple present tense.

English:

> I am Rahul.

Bangla:

> Ami Rahul.

Simple!

---

# Sentence Pattern 2

## My name is...

```
Amar naam ...
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Amar naam Rahul. | My name is Rahul. |
| Amar naam Anita. | My name is Anita. |
| Amar naam Srikanth. | My name is Srikanth. |

This is one of the first expressions every learner should master.

---

# Sentence Pattern 3

## I am fine.

```
Ami bhalo achhi.
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami bhalo achhi. | I am fine. |
| Ami khub bhalo achhi. | I am very well. |
| Ami bhalo nei. | I am not well. |

Useful replies:

**Question**

```
Apni bhalo achhen?
```

How are you?

**Answer**

```
Ami bhalo achhi.
```

I am fine.

---

# Sentence Pattern 4

## I have...

```
Amar ... ache.
```

Literally, this means:

> "With me ... exists."

Don't worry about the grammar.

Just remember the pattern.

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Amar boi ache. | I have a book. |
| Amar gari ache. | I have a car. |
| Amar bondhu ache. | I have a friend. |
| Amar shomoy ache. | I have time. |

Negative:

```
Amar shomoy nei.
```

I don't have time.

---

# Sentence Pattern 5

## I want...

```
Ami ... chai.
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami cha chai. | I want tea. |
| Ami pani chai. | I want water. |
| Ami boi chai. | I want a book. |
| Ami jete chai. | I want to go. |
| Ami khete chai. | I want to eat. |

This is one of the most useful patterns in everyday life.

---

# Sentence Pattern 6

## I like...

```
Amar ... bhalo lage.
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Amar cha bhalo lage. | I like tea. |
| Amar cinema bhalo lage. | I like movies. |
| Amar Bangla gaan bhalo lage. | I like Bengali songs. |
| Amar Kolkata bhalo lage. | I like Kolkata. |

Negative:

```
Amar eta bhalo lage na.
```

I don't like this.

---

# Sentence Pattern 7

## I know...

```
Ami jani.
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami jani. | I know. |
| Ami jani na. | I don't know. |
| Ami bujhi. | I understand. |
| Ami bujhi na. | I don't understand. |

These four expressions appear constantly in conversation.

---

# Sentence Pattern 8

## Please give me...

```
Amake ... din.
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Amake pani din. | Please give me water. |
| Amake cha din. | Please give me tea. |
| Amake boi din. | Please give me the book. |

Informal:

```
Amake dao.
```

Give it to me.

---

# Sentence Pattern 9

## Where is...?

```
... kothay?
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Station kothay? | Where is the station? |
| Amar boi kothay? | Where is my book? |
| Maa kothay? | Where is Mother? |
| Dokan kothay? | Where is the shop? |

One word—

**kothay**

—will become one of your best friends.

---

# Sentence Pattern 10

## What is this?

```
Eta ki?
```

Examples

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Eta ki? | What is this? |
| Ota ki? | What is that? |
| Eta boi. | This is a book. |
| Ota chair. | That is a chair. |

Simple and extremely useful.

# Everyday Conversation

### Meeting Someone

**A**

Nomoshkar.

Hello.

**B**

Nomoshkar.

Hello.

**A**

Apnar naam ki?

What is your name?

**B**

Amar naam Rahul.

My name is Rahul.

**A**

Apni bhalo achhen?

How are you?

**B**

Ami bhalo achhi.

I am fine.

Dhonnobad.

Thank you.

# Conversation at a Tea Stall

**Customer**

Nomoshkar.

Hello.

Cha ache?

Do you have tea?

**Shopkeeper**

Haa.

Yes.

**Customer**

Amake ek cup cha din.

Please give me one cup of tea.

**Shopkeeper**

Thik ache.

Okay.

# Common Beginner Mistakes

### ❌ Thinking in English

Don't translate word by word.

Learn the complete pattern instead.

Instead of remembering:

> want = chai

Remember:

> Ami ... chai.

---

### ❌ Memorizing Grammar Tables

Not necessary.

If you can say

```
Ami pani chai.
```

you are already communicating successfully.

Grammar will come naturally through repeated patterns.

---

### ❌ Being Afraid of Mistakes

Every Bengali speaker will understand you if you use these simple sentence patterns.

Confidence matters more than perfection.

# Summary

Excellent!

You can now create dozens of useful Bangla sentences using just a few simple patterns.

You learned how to:

- Introduce yourself
- Tell someone your name
- Say how you are
- Say what you have
- Ask for things
- Express likes
- Ask simple questions
- Hold your first short conversation

These sentence patterns appear constantly in everyday speech and will serve as the foundation for everything that follows.

In the next lesson, you'll learn the **essential Bangla verbs** that power almost every conversation. Once you master those verbs, you'll be able to talk about what you do, where you go, what you eat, who you meet, and much more.
