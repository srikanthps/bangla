# Part 10 – Listening Practice

# Welcome

This is where everything you've learned begins to come together.

Until now, you've studied pronunciation, vocabulary, sentence patterns, conversations, movie expressions, and song vocabulary.

Now it's time to **train your ears**.

Listening is a skill.

The more you practice, the better your brain becomes at recognizing familiar words, predicting meaning, and understanding complete conversations.

Remember:

You do **not** need to understand every word.

Your goal is to understand the **message**.

---

# How to Use This Chapter

For each dialogue:

1. Read the Bangla conversation aloud.
2. Read the English translation.
3. Read the conversation again without looking at the translation.
4. Repeat until you can understand the conversation directly in Bangla.

If possible, ask a Bengali-speaking friend to read the dialogues aloud.

# Dialogue 1 — Greeting a Neighbour

### Bangla

**Rahul**

Nomoshkar!

Kemon achhen?

**Neighbour**

Bhalo achhi.

Apni?

**Rahul**

Ami-o bhalo achhi.

**Neighbour**

Office jacchen?

**Rahul**

Haa.

Shondhay fire ashbo.

---

### English

**Rahul**

Hello!

How are you?

**Neighbour**

I'm fine.

And you?

**Rahul**

I'm fine too.

**Neighbour**

Going to the office?

**Rahul**

Yes.

I'll be back in the evening.

---

## Listen For

- kemon
- bhalo
- office
- shondha
- fire ashbo

# Building Listening Confidence

When you hear spoken Bangla, follow these five steps.

### Step 1

Recognize familiar words.

### Step 2

Identify the verb.

### Step 3

Notice the emotion.

### Step 4

Guess the missing information.

### Step 5

Keep listening.

Don't stop because of one unknown word.

---

# Listening Without Translation

This is one of the most important skills you'll develop.

Instead of thinking:

```
Ki...

means...

what...
```

Train your brain to immediately understand:

```
Ki?
```

as

"What?"

without mentally translating.

The same is true for:

- Dhonnobad.
- Thik ache.
- Cholo.
- Bhalo.
- Kothay?
- Keno?

Eventually, these expressions become automatic.

# Real-World Listening Practice

Try listening to Bangla every day, even for just ten minutes.

Good sources include:

- News clips
- Interviews
- Cooking videos
- Travel vlogs
- Children's stories
- Podcasts
- Movie scenes
- Television interviews

At first, don't worry about understanding everything.

Simply train your ears to become familiar with the rhythm and sound of the language.
