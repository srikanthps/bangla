# Part 7 – Movie Bangla

# Welcome

If you've reached this chapter, congratulations!

You now know enough spoken Bangla to begin understanding real movie dialogues.

The first time you watch a Bengali movie, it may seem like everyone is speaking too fast.

Don't worry.

Movies are actually one of the best ways to improve your listening skills because the same expressions appear again and again.

Family dramas, romantic films, comedies, thrillers, and even detective stories all use a surprisingly similar everyday vocabulary.

In this chapter, you'll learn the expressions that appear repeatedly in Bengali cinema.

---

# Movie Tip

Don't try to understand every sentence.

Instead, try to answer these questions while watching a scene.

- Who is speaking?
- Who are they speaking to?
- Are they happy, angry, worried, or excited?
- What just happened?
- Which familiar words did you hear?

If you can answer these questions, you're already following the story.

---

# Scene 1 – Meeting Someone

One of the first scenes in many films involves two people meeting.

Listen for expressions like:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Nomoshkar. | Hello. |
| Kemon acho? | How are you? |
| Kemon achhen? | How are you? (respectful) |
| Ki khobor? | What's new? |
| Kothay chile? | Where were you? |
| Eto din kothay chile? | Where have you been all this time? |
| Onek din por dekha. | Nice to see you after so long. |

---

# Scene 2 – Family Conversations

Family members speak differently from strangers.

Listen for these expressions.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki korchho? | What are you doing? |
| Kothay jaccho? | Where are you going? |
| Kakhon ashbe? | When will you come back? |
| Kheye nao. | Eat your meal. |
| Taratari esho. | Come quickly. |
| Bhalo kore poro. | Study well. |
| Shunte pachho? | Are you listening? |

These lines appear in countless family dramas.

---

# Scene 3 – Friends Talking

Friends usually speak casually.

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki re? | Hey! |
| Kemon achhish? | How are you? |
| Chol. | Come on. |
| Chol jai. | Let's go. |
| Ki bolchhish? | What are you saying? |
| Darun! | Awesome! |
| Oshadharon! | Fantastic! |

Notice that conversations between friends are often short and energetic.

---

# Scene 4 – Romance

Romantic movies reuse many of the same expressions.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami tomake bhalobashi. | I love you. |
| Ami tomake miss kori. | I miss you. |
| Tumi amar shobkichhu. | You are my everything. |
| Tumi khub shundor. | You are very beautiful. |
| Tumi ashbe? | Will you come? |
| Amar opekkha koro. | Wait for me. |
| Ami fire ashbo. | I will come back. |

Don't worry about remembering every sentence.

You'll hear these patterns repeatedly.

---

# Scene 5 – Arguments

Arguments sound fast, but the vocabulary is often simple.

Listen for:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki holo? | What happened? |
| Ki bolchho? | What are you saying? |
| Eta thik na. | This isn't right. |
| Ami bishwas kori na. | I don't believe it. |
| Amar dosh noy. | It's not my fault. |
| Chup koro! | Be quiet! |
| Jao! | Go away! |

The emotions help you understand the dialogue even if you miss a few words.

---

# Scene 6 – Emotional Moments

These expressions appear whenever characters comfort each other.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Chinta koro na. | Don't worry. |
| Shob thik hoye jabe. | Everything will be alright. |
| Ami achhi. | I'm here. |
| Bhoy peyo na. | Don't be afraid. |
| Tumi eka noy. | You're not alone. |
| Ami bujhte parchhi. | I understand. |

These are useful expressions in real life too.

---

# Scene 7 – Comedy

Comedy often relies on tone more than vocabulary.

Common reactions include:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki? | What? |
| Sotti? | Really? |
| Na re! | No way! |
| Are baba! | Oh my goodness! |
| E ki! | What's this! |
| Darun! | Great! |

Watch the actors' expressions carefully.

Sometimes the facial expression tells the story before the words do.

---

# Scene 8 – Detective and Thriller Movies

Mystery films repeat certain expressions.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ke? | Who? |
| Kothay? | Where? |
| Kokhon? | When? |
| Keno? | Why? |
| Praman ache? | Is there evidence? |
| Ami dekhechi. | I saw it. |
| Ami jani. | I know. |
| Keu dekheni. | Nobody saw it. |

If you enjoy detective stories, learning these expressions will help enormously.

# Words That Reveal Emotion

Sometimes you only need one word.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|

| Kharap | Bad |
| Dukkho | Sadness |
| Khushi | Happiness |
| Rag | Anger |

These emotional words appear constantly in films.

# Movie Conversation

### Two Friends

**Arjun**

Ki re?

Kothay chhilish?

*Hey! Where were you?*

**Rohan**

Office-e.

Onek kaj chhilo.

*At the office. There was a lot of work.*

**Arjun**

Tahole chol.

Cha khai.

*Then let's go have some tea.*

**Rohan**

Darun!

*Great!*

Notice how much of this conversation you already understand.

# Guessing Unknown Words

Imagine you hear:

> Ami kal Kolkata jachhi.

Suppose you only recognize:

- Ami
- Kal
- Jachhi

You can still guess:

"I'm going somewhere tomorrow."

Later, when someone mentions Kolkata, your brain automatically fills in the missing piece.

This is exactly how children learn language.

---

# Watching Without Pausing

Many beginners pause every few seconds.

Don't.

Instead:

Watch five minutes continuously.

Accept that you'll miss some words.

Focus on the overall story.

You'll understand more than you think.

---

# Listening Challenge

You'll probably hear them dozens of times.

# Movie Challenge

Watch a Bengali movie of your choice.

After watching one scene, ask yourself:

- Did I understand who was speaking?
- Did I understand the emotion?
- Which words did I recognize?
- Could I guess the meaning of unknown words?

If the answer is "yes" to most of these questions, you're making excellent progress.

Remember, understanding **70%** is enough to enjoy a movie.

The remaining **30%** will gradually disappear as your vocabulary grows.
