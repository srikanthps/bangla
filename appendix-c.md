# Appendix C – 1,000 High-Frequency Spoken Bangla Words

# Welcome

Congratulations on completing the course!

This appendix contains approximately **1,000 of the most frequently used words in spoken Bangla**.

Unlike a dictionary, these words are grouped by topic so they are easier to learn and remember.

You do **not** need to memorize every word.

Instead:

- Learn one topic at a time.
- Speak each word aloud.
- Try using the words in your own sentences.
- Review them regularly.

By the time you become familiar with most of these words, you'll understand a large percentage of everyday spoken Bangla.

# Cultural Note

In Bengali culture, greetings are often followed by a short conversation.

Instead of immediately discussing business or asking for help, people commonly ask:

- Kemon achhen?
- Ki khobor?
- Bari-te shobai bhalo?

This small talk helps create warmth and connection before moving on to the main conversation.

# A Note About "Kal"

One word often confuses beginners:

**Kal** can mean **yesterday** or **tomorrow**.

For example:

*I will go to Kolkata tomorrow.*

The verb tells you whether the speaker is talking about the past or the future, so native speakers rarely find it confusing.
