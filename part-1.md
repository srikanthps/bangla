# Part 1 – Bangla Pronunciation

# Welcome

Before learning words and sentences, let's spend a little time understanding how Bangla sounds.

The good news is that Bangla pronunciation is quite regular. Once you learn the sounds, you'll be able to pronounce most words correctly just by reading their Roman transliteration.

You do **not** need to learn the Bengali script for this course.

Throughout the course, all Bangla examples will be written using the English alphabet.

---

# Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

- Read Romanized Bangla confidently.
- Pronounce the most common Bangla sounds.
- Avoid the pronunciation mistakes made by many beginners.
- Speak your first Bangla words naturally.

---

# A Simple Rule

Say Bangla exactly as it is written.

Unlike English, where the same letters often sound different—

- though
- through
- rough
- cough

—Bangla pronunciation is much more consistent.

---

# The Five Most Important Vowels

## A

Usually sounds like the **u** in **but**.

Examples

| Bangla | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| ami | uh-mee | I |
| bhalo | bha-lo | good |
| baba | baa-baa | father |

---

## Aa

A long "a" as in **father**.

Examples

| Bangla | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| maa | maa | mother |
| naam | naam | name |
| kaaj | kaaj | work |

---

## I

Like **ee** in **see**.

Examples

| Bangla | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| didi | dee-dee | elder sister |
| tinti | tin-tee | three |

---

## O

Like "o" in **go**.

Examples

| Bangla | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| bolo | bo-lo | speak |
| koro | ko-ro | do |

---

## U

Like "oo" in **food**.

Examples

| Bangla | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| tumi | too-mee | you |
| shuno | shu-no | listen |

---

# Common Consonants

Most consonants are pronounced almost exactly as they are in English.

| Letter | Example | Meaning |
|---------|----------|---------|
| b | baba | father |
| m | maa | mother |
| p | pani | water |
| n | naam | name |
| l | lok | person |
| g | golpo | story |
| k | kotha | word, conversation |

---

# The "Bh" Sound

The letters **bh** are pronounced as a soft **b** with a puff of air.

Examples

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| bhalo | good |
| bhat | cooked rice |
| bhai | brother |

Do **not** pronounce it like the English word "bee-h".

It is one sound.

---

# The "Dh" Sound

Similarly,

**dh**

is one sound.

Examples

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| dhonnobad | thank you |
| dhore | hold |

---

# The "Sh" Sound

Bangla has several letters that are written differently in the Bengali script but are usually pronounced the same in everyday speech.

For this course, we simply write:

**sh**

Examples

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| shuno | listen |
| shob | all |
| shundor | beautiful |

---

# The "Ch" Sound

Like "ch" in **chair**.

Examples

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| cha | tea |
| cholo | let's go |
| chhoto | small |

---

# The "R" Sound

The Bangla **r** is usually softer than the English **r**.

Don't exaggerate it.

Examples

- raat
- rasta
- rong

Simply keep it light.

---

# Words Are Usually Pronounced Fully

English often swallows sounds.

Bangla usually doesn't.

For example:

```
A-mi
```

not

```
Am
```

Similarly,

```
Tu-mi
```

not

```
Tum
```

Say every syllable clearly.

---

# Word Stress

Unlike English, Bangla does **not** strongly stress one syllable.

Instead, every syllable receives almost equal importance.

English:

**TA-ble**

Bangla:

**bha-lo**

Both syllables sound nearly equal.

This gives Bangla its smooth, musical rhythm.

---

# Rhythm

Listen to native speakers and notice how naturally the language flows.

Avoid speaking Bangla with heavy English stress.

Instead, think of each word as a gentle sequence of syllables.

Example:

```
A-mi Bang-la shi-kchhi.
```

Not

```
AMI bangla SHIKCHHI.
```

# Listening Tip

Don't worry if you don't understand every word when listening to Bangla.

For now, focus only on:

- recognizing familiar sounds
- hearing where one word ends and the next begins
- identifying words you already know

Your brain will naturally become better at separating spoken words with practice.
