# Part 9 – Speaking Naturally

# Welcome

By now, you know hundreds of useful words, common verbs, everyday conversations, movie expressions, and song vocabulary.

People will understand you.

Now it's time to make your Bangla sound **more natural**.

Native speakers don't just choose the right words—they choose the right **tone**, **level of politeness**, and **expressions** for each situation.

In this chapter, you'll learn how Bengali speakers actually interact with family, friends, strangers, colleagues, and elders.

This is the difference between **speaking Bangla** and **speaking like a Bengali**.

---

# The Three Ways to Say "You"

One of the most important features of Bangla is choosing the right form of **"you."**

| Bangla | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|
| tui | Very close friends, siblings, young children. Very informal. |
| tumi | Friends, classmates, colleagues, most everyday conversations. |
| apni | Elders, strangers, teachers, customers, formal situations. |

Choosing the right one immediately makes your speech sound natural.

---

# When in Doubt, Use "Apni"

If you're speaking with someone you don't know well:

Use:

```
Apni kemon achhen?
```

Not:

```
Tui kemon achhish?
```

Being too polite is rarely considered rude.

Being too informal with the wrong person can be.

---

# Speaking with Friends

Friends usually use relaxed language.

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki re? | Hey! |
| Ki korchhish? | What are you doing? |
| Chol! | Let's go! |
| Darun! | Awesome! |
| Thik ache. | Okay. |

Notice how conversations are often short.

---

# Speaking with Elders

With elders, soften your speech.

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Apni boshun. | Please sit. |
| Apni cha khaben? | Would you like tea? |
| Dhonnobad. | Thank you. |
| Ashun. | Please come in. |
| Apni bhalo achhen? | How are you? |

Politeness is shown more through word choice than through long sentences.

---

# Showing Respect

Small changes make a big difference.

Instead of saying:

```
Bosho.
```

Say:

```
Boshun.
```

Instead of:

```
Khao.
```

Say:

```
Khaben?
```

Instead of:

```
Eso.
```

Say:

```
Ashun.
```

These respectful forms are heard constantly in homes, shops, and workplaces.

---

# Saying "Please"

Unlike English, Bangla doesn't always require a separate word for **please**.

Politeness often comes from tone and respectful verb forms.

For example:

```
Ek cup cha din.
```

One cup of tea, please.

No separate word for "please" is needed.

---

# Agreeing Naturally

Instead of always saying "yes," Bengali speakers often use expressions like:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Thik ache. | Okay. |
| Oboshshoi. | Certainly. |
| Haa. | Yes. |
| Nishchoy. | Definitely. |
| Tai. | That's right. |

---

# Disagreeing Politely

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Na. | No. |
| Amar mone hoy na. | I don't think so. |
| Hoyto na. | Maybe not. |
| Eta thik noy. | That's not right. |
| Ami ekmot noi. | I don't agree. |

Notice that disagreement is often expressed gently.

---

# Expressing Surprise

You'll hear these expressions frequently.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Sotti? | Really? |
| Tai naki? | Is that so? |
| Are baba! | Oh my goodness! |
| E ki! | What's this! |
| Ashchorjo! | Amazing! |

These expressions are especially common in movies.

---

# Expressing Happiness

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Darun! | Great! |
| Khub bhalo! | Very good! |
| Oshadharon! | Wonderful! |
| Darun khobor! | Great news! |
| Besh! | Excellent! |

---

# Expressing Sadness

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Kharap lagchhe. | I feel bad. |
| Dukkho pelam. | I'm saddened. |
| Koshto hochhe. | It hurts. |
| Ki dukkher kotha! | How sad! |

---

# Expressing Gratitude

There are many ways to thank someone.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Dhonnobad. | Thank you. |
| Onek dhonnobad. | Thank you very much. |
| Apnake onek dhonnobad. | Thank you very much (respectful). |

Often, a warm smile is just as important as the words.

---

# Apologizing

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Dukkhito. | Sorry. |
| Khoma korun. | Please forgive me. |
| Amar bhul hoyeche. | I made a mistake. |
| Ami dukkhito. | I'm sorry. |

---

# Asking Someone to Wait

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ektu darao. | Wait a moment. |
| Ektu opekkha korun. | Please wait a moment. |
| Ek minute. | One minute. |

You'll hear these expressions in every workplace and shop.

---

# Ending Conversations Naturally

Instead of simply saying goodbye, Bengali speakers often use expressions like:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Tahole ami jai. | Well then, I'll leave. |
| Abar dekha hobe. | See you again. |
| Bhalo thakben. | Take care. (Respectful) |
| Bhalo theko. | Take care. (Informal) |

These endings sound much more natural than a simple "bye."

---

# Small Talk

Here's a conversation that sounds very natural.

**Rahul**

Ki khobor?

*What's new?*

**Amit**

Shob bhalo.

Tomar?

*Everything's good. Yours?*

**Rahul**

Bhalo.

Kaj niye byasto.

*Good. Busy with work.*

**Amit**

Tahole pore kotha hobe.

*Then let's talk later.*

**Rahul**

Thik ache.

*Okay.*

Notice how short and relaxed the conversation is.

---

# The Power of Tone

Listen to these two sentences.

```
Thik ache.
```

Depending on the tone, it can mean:

- Okay.
- Fine.
- Sure.
- I agree.
- That's enough.
- Let's move on.

Native speakers rely heavily on tone rather than adding extra words.

---

# Cultural Note — Hospitality

If you visit a Bengali home, you may hear:

```
Cha khaben?
```

Would you like tea?

If you politely decline, don't be surprised if you're asked again!

Offering food and tea is a traditional way of welcoming guests. Accepting at least a little is often appreciated, though a polite refusal is perfectly acceptable if you truly don't want anything.

---

# Conversation

### Visiting a Friend

**Host**

Ashun!

Bhetore ashun.

*Please come in!*

**Guest**

Dhonnobad.

*KThank you.*

**Host**

Boshun.

Cha khaben?

*Please sit. Would you like some tea?*

**Guest**

Haa.

Dhonnobad.

*Yes. Thank you.*

**Host**

Biscuit neben?

**Guest**

*Certainly.*

# Speaking Tip

Don't try to sound perfect.

Instead, try to sound **warm**.

A smile, polite tone, and a few respectful expressions will make your Bangla sound far more natural than using complicated vocabulary.

People appreciate sincere communication much more than perfect grammar.

# Summary

Excellent!

You now know how Bengali speakers naturally:

- Show respect.
- Speak with friends.
- Speak with elders.
- Agree and disagree politely.
- Express emotions.
- Thank people.
- Apologize.
- End conversations naturally.

These small details make a huge difference in everyday communication.
