# Part 5 – Everyday Conversations

# Welcome

Congratulations!

You now know enough vocabulary and verbs to begin having real conversations.

This chapter is where Bangla starts becoming a living language instead of a collection of words.

Rather than learning isolated sentences, you'll learn complete conversations that you can use immediately.

Read every dialogue aloud.

If possible, practice with a friend or language partner.

Don't worry if you don't understand every word yet. Focus on understanding the conversation as a whole.

---

# Conversation 1 — Meeting Someone for the First Time

**Rahul**

Nomoshkar.

*Hello.*

**Anita**

Nomoshkar.

*Hello.*

**Rahul**

Amar naam Rahul.

*My name is Rahul.*

**Anita**

Amar naam Anita.

*My name is Anita.*

**Rahul**

Apni kemon achhen?

*How are you?*

**Anita**

Ami bhalo achhi.

Apni?

*I am fine. And you?*

**Rahul**

Ami-o bhalo achhi.

*I am also fine.*

---

## Useful Words

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| nomoshkar | hello |
| naam | name |
| kemon | how |
| bhalo | fine, good |
| ami-o | me too / I also |

# Conversation 2 — Meeting a Friend

**Amit**

Kemon achho?

*Hey Rahul! How are you?*

**Rahul**

Bhalo achhi.

*I'm fine. You?*

**Amit**

Ami-o bhalo.

*I'm also fine.*

**Rahul**

Ki khobor?

*What's new?*

**Amit**

Shob bhalo.

*Everything is good.*

# Conversation 3 — At Home

**Mother**

Rahul!

Kothay jaccho?

*Where are you going?*

**Rahul**

Ami bajare jacchi.

*I'm going to the market.*

**Mother**

Ki kinbe?

*What will you buy?*

**Rahul**

Shobji aar fol.

*Vegetables and fruit.*

**Mother**

Thik ache.

Taratari esho.

*Okay. Come back soon.*

---

| shobji | vegetables |
| fol | fruit |

| esho | come |

# Conversation 4 — Buying Tea

**Customer**

Nomoshkar.

Ek cup cha din.

*Hello. One cup of tea, please.*

**Shopkeeper**

Chini deben?

*Would you like sugar?*

**Customer**

Haa, ektu.

*Yes, a little.*

**Shopkeeper**

Ar kichhu?

*Anything else?*

**Customer**

Na.

Dhonnobad.

*No. Thank you.*

---

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| ek cup | one cup |
| chini | sugar |
| ektu | a little |
| ar kichhu | anything else |

# Conversation 5 — At a Restaurant

**Waiter**

Apni ki khaben?

*What would you like to eat?*

**Customer**

Ami bhat aar machh chai.

*I'd like rice and fish.*

**Waiter**

Ar kichhu?

*Anything else?*

**Customer**

Ek glass pani din.

*Please give me a glass of water.*

**Waiter**

Oboshshoi.

*Certainly.*

---

|---------|----------|

| pani | water |

# Conversation 6 — Asking for Directions

**Traveller**

Station kothay?

*Where is the station?*

**Local Person**

Oi rastay shoja jaan.

*Go straight on that road.*

Tarpor baam dike ghurun.

*Then turn left.*

Station okhanei.

*The station is there.*

**Traveller**

Dhonnobad.

*Thank you.*

---

## Direction Words

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| shoja | straight |
| baam | left |
| dan | right |
| rasta | road |
| ghurun | turn |

# Cultural Note — Tea is More Than a Drink

If you spend time with Bengali speakers, you'll quickly notice how often people ask:

> **Cha khaben?**
>
> *Would you like some tea?*

Tea is more than a beverage—it's a way of welcoming guests, starting conversations, taking breaks, and spending time together.

Don't be surprised if many conversations begin over a cup of tea.
