# Learn Spoken Bangla Through English

## Understand Conversations • Enjoy Movies • Sing Along with Songs

> **A practical spoken Bangla course for English speakers.**
>
> Learn to understand and speak everyday Bangla naturally—without first learning the Bengali script or memorizing complicated grammar.

---

# Welcome

Most language books begin with the alphabet, grammar rules, and long vocabulary lists.

This course takes a different approach.

The goal is simple:

> **Help you understand spoken Bangla and communicate confidently in everyday situations.**

Whether you want to talk with Bengali friends, understand Bengali movies, enjoy Bengali songs, or travel comfortably in West Bengal or Bangladesh, this course is designed to get you there as quickly as possible.

Learning the Bengali script is **optional** and included only as an appendix for learners who wish to read books, newspapers, or song lyrics later.

---

# Who Is This Course For?

This course is ideal for:

- English speakers learning Bangla for the first time.
- Indians who already know English and another Indian language.
- People with Bengali friends, colleagues, or relatives.
- Fans of Bengali cinema and music.
- Travelers visiting Bengali-speaking regions.
- Heritage learners who understand a little Bangla but want to speak confidently.

---

# What You'll Learn

By the end of this course, you'll be able to:

- Introduce yourself confidently.
- Hold everyday conversations.
- Ask and answer common questions.
- Understand most slow and normal spoken Bangla.
- Recognize common expressions used in Bengali movies.
- Understand the meaning of many Bengali songs.
- Continue improving through conversations, movies, and music.

---

# How This Course Works

Instead of teaching grammar first, this course teaches **patterns**.

You'll learn:

- The words people actually use.
- The sentences people actually speak.
- The expressions you hear in movies.
- The vocabulary that appears repeatedly in songs.

Grammar is introduced only when it helps you communicate more naturally.

# How to Study

For the best results:

1. Study one lesson at a time.
2. Speak every example aloud.
3. Repeat the dialogues several times.
4. Listen to native speakers whenever possible.
5. Review previous lessons regularly.
6. Don't worry about making mistakes—focus on communicating.

Even **15–20 minutes a day** is enough to make steady progress.

---

# Roman Transliteration

Throughout this course, Bangla is written using the English alphabet (Roman transliteration), so you can start speaking immediately.

Example:

**Bangla (Roman):**

`Ami Bangla shikchhi.`

**English:**

*I am learning Bangla.*

No knowledge of the Bengali script is required.
