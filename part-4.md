# Part 4 – Essential Verbs

# Welcome

If vocabulary is the building block of a language, **verbs are its engine**.

Most everyday Bangla conversations use a surprisingly small number of verbs. Once you become comfortable with them, you'll begin understanding a large portion of what people say.

This lesson focuses on the **20 most important Bangla verbs**. Don't worry about memorizing every possible form. Instead, learn how these verbs are used in real conversations.

By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to talk about what you do, where you go, what you eat, what you see, and much more.

---

# A Quick Note About Verbs

Every Bangla verb has a dictionary form ending in **-a**.

Examples:

- kora (to do)
- jawa (to go)
- asha (to come)
- khaoa (to eat)

In everyday conversation, however, you'll hear different forms of the verb depending on who is speaking and when the action happens.

For example:

| Dictionary Form | Everyday Speech | Meaning |
|-----------------|----------------|---------|
| kora | kori | I do |
| jawa | jai | I go |
| asha | ashi | I come |
| khaoa | khai | I eat |

Don't try to memorize everything today. Focus on recognizing the patterns.

---

# 1. Kora — To Do

This is probably the most useful verb in Bangla.

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami kaj kori. | I work. *(Literally: I do work.)* |
| Ami porashona kori. | I study. |
| Tumi ki korcho? | What are you doing? |
| Ami kichhu korchhi na. | I am not doing anything. |

Useful words:

- kaj = work
- porashona = study
- ki = what

---

# 2. Jawa — To Go

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami jai. | I go. |
| Ami school jai. | I go to school. |
| Ami office jai. | I go to the office. |
| Cholo jai. | Let's go. |

You'll hear **cholo** in almost every Bengali movie.

---

# 3. Asha — To Come

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami ashi. | I come. |
| Tumi ashbe? | Will you come? |
| Kakhon ashbe? | When will you come? |
| Ami ekhon ashchhi. | I'm coming now. |

---

# 4. Khaoa — To Eat

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami khai. | I eat. |
| Ami bhat khai. | I eat rice. |
| Tumi ki khabe? | What will you eat? |
| Cholo khete jai. | Let's go eat. |

Useful words:

- bhat = cooked rice
- khete = to eat

---

# 5. Pan Kora — To Drink

Although the dictionary form is *pan kora*, in daily conversation people often use forms of the verb **khaoa** for drinking tea, coffee, milk and many beverages.

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami cha khai. | I drink tea. |
| Ami coffee khai. | I drink coffee. |
| Ami pani khai. | I drink water. *(Common in many regions.)* |
| Tumi cha khabe? | Will you have tea? |

Don't be surprised if you hear **khai** used for both eating and drinking.

Examples:

|---------|----------|
| Ami boli. | I say. |

| Ami Bangla boli. | I speak Bangla. |

Examples:

|---------|----------|
| Ami shuni. | I listen. |

| Ami gaan shunchhi. | I am listening to music. |

Useful word:

# 8. Dekha — To See / To Watch

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami dekhi. | I see. |

| Tumi ki dekhcho? | What are you watching? |

# 9. Dewa — To Give

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Amake pani din. | Please give me water. |
| Amake boi din. | Please give me the book. |
| Ami tomake debo. | I will give you. |

---

# 10. Newa — To Take

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami nebo. | I will take it. |
| Eta nin. | Please take this. |
| Ami boi nilam. | I took the book. |

---

# 11. Jana — To Know

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami jani. | I know. |
| Ami jani na. | I don't know. |
| Tumi jano? | Do you know? |

---

# 12. Bojha — To Understand

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami bujhi. | I understand. |
| Ami bujhi na. | I don't understand. |
| Ekhon bujhlam. | Now I understand. |

---

# 13. Thaka — To Stay / To Live

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami Bengaluru-te thaki. | I live in Bengaluru. |
| Tumi kothay thako? | Where do you live? |
| Ami ekhane thaki. | I live here. |

---

# 14. Bhalobasha — To Love

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami Bangla bhalobashi. | I love Bangla. |
| Ami gaan bhalobashi. | I love songs. |
| Ami tomake bhalobashi. | I love you. |

One of the most recognizable Bangla expressions.

---

# 15. Chawa — To Want

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami eta chai. | I want this. |
| Ami cha chai. | I want tea. |
| Tumi ki chao? | What do you want? |

---

# 16. Para — Can / Be Able To

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ami pari. | I can. |
| Ami Bangla bolte pari. | I can speak Bangla. |
| Ami pari na. | I can't. |

You'll hear **pari** and **pari na** very often.

---

# 17. Laga — To Feel / To Seem / To Be Needed

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Amar bhalo lage. | I like it. |
| Koto taka lagbe? | How much will it cost? |
| Shomoy lagbe. | It will take time. |

This verb has many meanings depending on context.

---

# 18. Howa — To Become / To Happen / To Be

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki holo? | What happened? |
| Thik hobe. | It will be okay. |
| Ki hochhe? | What is happening? |

You'll hear **Ki holo?** countless times in Bengali movies.

---

# 19. Rakha — To Keep

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Eta ekhane rakho. | Keep this here. |
| Ami boi rekhechi. | I have kept the book. |

---

# 20. Ana — To Bring

Examples:

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Pani ano. | Bring water. |
| Ami niye ashchhi. | I'm bringing it. |

---

# Expressions You'll Hear Every Day

Memorize these.

| Bangla | English |
|---------|----------|
| Ki korcho? | What are you doing? |
| Kothay jaccho? | Where are you going? |
| Kakhon ashbe? | When will you come? |
| Ki khabe? | What will you eat? |
| Ki holo? | What happened? |
| Tumi jano? | Do you know? |
| Ami bujhi na. | I don't understand. |
| Ami pari. | I can. |
| Ami pari na. | I can't. |
| Cholo jai. | Let's go. |

If you understand these ten expressions, you'll recognize them repeatedly in conversations, films, and television.

# Mini Conversation

### Two Friends Meeting

**Rahul**

Ki korcho?

*What are you doing?*

**Amit**

Ami porashona korchhi.

*I am studying.*

**Rahul**

Cha khabe?

*Would you like some tea?*

**Amit**

Haa.

Cholo.

*Yes. Let's go.*
